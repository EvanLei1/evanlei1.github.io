Los Angeles Outer Area Bus List

Ventura County

Gold Coast Transit

Serves Ventura County, with 17 NABI 35-LFW buses, 26 New Flyer C40LFR buses, and 39 Gillig Low Floor CNG buses.

Thousand Oaks Transit

Serves the city of Thousand Oaks. One of a few Orion VII NG buses left in service, and the only one in Southern California. A few Gillig BRT CNG units were used around the city.

Simi Valley Transit

The fleet is entirely based on New Flyer Xcelsior natural gas buses, with 11 in total, including 3 40' buses and 8 35' ones.

Moorpark City Transit

Operated by Thousand Oaks Transit that serves Moorpark City, there were a few ElDorado National E-Z Rider II CNG buses.

Ventura County Transportation Commission

Intercity service around the county including Santa Barbara. It uses mostly MCI buses with a few Volvo 9700 buses. An order of 5 BYD C10M was not delivered yet.

San Bernardino County

Omnitrans

All of the buses were made by New Flyer. There were a lot of 2009 New Flyer C40LFRs in the 1200 series and a huge set of XN40s. Four electric and hydrogen electric buses were used in 2021 and 2024 respectively. The sbX service uses 15 2012 and 2017 New Flyer XN60 buses with 5 doors while the purple line has 18 2024 New Flyer XE40 buses with 3 doors.

Morongo Basin Transit Authority

Mostly using Ford buses with a few high floor ElDorado National buses and two Gillig Low Floor CNG buses.

Victor Valley Transit Authority

Serves the area between Apple Valley and Barstow, with intercity service to San Bernardino and Fort Irwin NTC. Most of the buses were made by ENC except for New Flyer electric buses and 5 MCI buses.

Riveside County

Riverside Transit Agency

Active Fleet

Banning Transit System

Serves the city of Banning. There were some Ford buses and ENC E-Z Rider II BRT 32' CNG buses in service.

Beaumont Transit

Serves the city of Beaumont. There were some Ford and Chevrolet buses as well as some ENC buses.

Corona Cruiser

Although the city has Ford buses, there were 7 ElDorado National E-Z Rider II BRT 32' buses.

SunLine Transit Agency

Serves Palm Springs, Indio, and Cathedral City. Most of these buses were New Flyer XHE40 or XN40 with some BYD buses, MCI buses, and ElDorado National Axess FC bus. There were 3 MCI buses for San Bernardino service. 15 ex-RTA Gillig buses were transferred to here. There were a few New Flyer C40LFs in this area.

Orange County

Orange County Transportation Agency

Anaheim Resort Transportation

Serves the city of Anaheim. Most of the buses were BYD electric buses except for 3 ElDorado National buses.

San Diego County

North County Transit District

The entire fleet was based on New Flyer buses except for ex-MTS 3000 series Ford buses, used for the Coaster Connection routes. Four 2000 New Flyer D40LF buses remain in service, along with 2007 C40LF buses. Most of the units after 2012 were XN40s with the exception of 20 2019-20 XN35s in the 1900 series and 12 2024 XHE40 buses.
